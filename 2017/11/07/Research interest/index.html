Analysis and Optimization of High Power Wireless Charging Vehicle System

Student Research Training (SRT) Program, Tsinghua University
Supervised by Prof. Fuyuan Yang

　　In this project we conducted an investigation on the operating cycle of a commercial electric bus which is equipped with wireless charging system in　Changsha, Hunan Province, China.

　　Currently, the buses are designed to operate in daytime and get charged at night. In order to explore the battery downsizing potential, optimization of the buses’ departure schedule and charging schedule is necessary. According to our results, the rated battery capacity can be reduced by more than 70% if extra charging opportunities are introduced to daily operation.

　　My contribution is quantifying different phases during the bus’s daily operation based on field test data and then modeling its operating cycle in MATLAB. The following figures reflect the SOC(state of charge) analysis which is my first step to build that model.

Design Space Exploration for Heavy-duty Series Plug-in Hybrid Electric Truck

CERC-TRUCK Project, Center for Automotive Research, the Ohio State University
Supervised by Prof. Giorgio Rizzoni， Dr. Qadeer Ahmed and Dr. Daniel Jung

　　Choosing a hybrid powertrain architecture, proper component sizes and control method is an important task to find the optimal trade-off between fuel economy, drivability,vehicle cost and other evaluation parameters.

　　In this project, I completed a visualized general framework for design space exploration of HEV, including a system-level design space (topology-design-control), input sector, constraint sector, design optimization algorithm sector, cost function sector and output sector.

　　Based on this framework, we chose the corresponding parts according to our research requirements. A search algorithm, using Gaussian Processes, is proposed that explores multiple architecture options, to identify the Pareto-optimal solutions.

　　The search algorithm is designed to carefully select the candidate in each iteration which is most likely to be Pareto-optimal, based on the results from previous candidates, to reduce computational time.

Experimental Study on Vehicular Dual-recirculation PEMFC System

State Key Laboratory of Automotive Safety and Energy, Tsinghua University
Supervised by Prof. Jianqiu Li and Ass.Prof. Liangfei Xu

　　Durability and reliability are still bottlenecks of vehicular polymer electrolyte membrane fuel cell (PEMFC) systems. In this study, dynamic performances and stable characteristics of a vehicular PEMFC system with dual exhaust gas recirculation will be studied.

　　In the next step, orthogonal test method is adopted to evaluate the main effects and interaction effects of different control parameters on system performance under varying operation conditions. Experimental data is also going to be used to calibrate the Simulink model of this system. Both the experimental and simulating approach will be used to explore two expected functions of this system: the voltage clamping in low current density, and the self-humidification without any external humidifiers.
