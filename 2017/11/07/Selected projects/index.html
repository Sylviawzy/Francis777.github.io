A LTP-based Future Urban Transport System under Fully Autonomous Vehicle Assumption

Course Project of Future Transportation (Fall 2017)

Supervised by Prof. Shengbo Eben Li

　　Reducing conflict points at intersections may reduce congestion and improve traffic effciency. In order to eliminate conflict points at intersection, we proposed two approaches:

  1. Combining left-turn prohibition policy with over bridge;
  2. Implementing reservation-based intersection control strategies on connected automated vehicles;

　　In this project we mainly explored the first approach. We first built VISSIM models of conventional intersections and LTP intersections based on the real-world traffic data of Hell’s Kitchen, Manhattan. Then we implemented LTP-bridge intersection on one road (9th Ave) while remaining others as normal LTP intersections. The result is significant as this simulation demo shows:

　　There are other optimization methods worth discussing: the connectivity of CAV makes it possible for predictive route planning optimization. By collecting vehicles’ route planning data in advance, we can “predict” the traffic burden on each section of roads before they enter this area, then we can reorganize certain vehicles’ route planning in order to transfer the burden from road sections with darker color to those with lighter color as is shown in the following figure.

Amputation stump cavity automatic design and molding

Course Project of Mechanical Design (Fall 2017)

Supervised by Prof. Linhong Ji

　　In this project I designed a set of medical equipment that can realize the automatical design and molding of amputation stump cavity. The whole process is divided into four steps:

  1. Center alignment of amputee
  2. Automatic molding of amputation stump cavity
  3. Scanning the die cavity
  4. 3D printing

　　The design is completed using Solidworks and AutoCAD. Certain properties of this equipment, including strength, stiffness and stability are also verified. For example, the right half of the figure below shows verification for overturning moment. The result proves that normal use will not cause any failure and instabiliby.

Policies Dealing with Traffic Congestion: Comparison between Beijing and Kolkata

Seminar Report of Technical English Communication (Summer 2016)

Supervised by Prof. Jianbo Zhang and Ass.Prof. Zhe Li

　　Technical English Communication is a three-week summer semester held by my department.

　　In our group project, we chose “urban traffic congestion” as our topic and and used Beijing and Kolkata (a city in Inida) as our comparing objects. We were selected to give a 12-min report on the final seminar of this class.

　　Our reports mainly covers the following four aspects:

  1. Background of urban transport situations in china and india;
  2. Comparative overview of traffic congestion problem in two China and Kolkta;
  3. Analysis of two cities’ urban transport policies which helps to prevent traffic congestion;
  4. Conclusion.

Influence of Different Alloying Elements on Low-alloy Steel’s Seawater Corrosion Resistance

Final Project of Engineering Materials (Spring 2016)

Supervised by Dr. Yang Shao

　　Different alloying elements may have various impacts on the corrosion resistance of steel. In this project paper, I discussed the following three questions based on the published results in previous papers:

  1. The common experimental method of analyzing alloying element’s impact on corrosion resistance of steel;

  2. Impacts of four alloying elements: copper, chromium, niobium and manganese;

  3. Correlation between the corrosion rate of steels and the content of added alloy element.
